Unit 3 - Theory Elements
There are several theoretical concepts and definitions in this section. It is important to have a clear idea of the following definitions.
  1. Be able to distinguish between a population and a sample.
  2. Be able to distinguish between parameter values and statistics, and know how those values are related to populations and samples.
  3. Be able to distinguish between Quantitative data and Qualitative data.
    • (Remark: For our own purposes, conside the differences between Numeric data and Categorical data.)
  4. Be able to distinguish between discrete numeric variables and continuous variables.
Remark: Scales of Measurement are useful to know, but I am not placing as much emphasis on that section of unit .

Unit 4 - Graphical Procedures
A lot of Material in this section related to constructing graphics in Minitab. Aside from Minitab, all I really want for you is to be able to understand and interpret the various types of charts.
  1. I would like you to be able to understand a statistical frequency table (see: Table 4.2 Blemish / Flash etc).
  2. (Remark: You will not be required to construct such a table by hand, in an exam situation.)
  3. While I would like you to be able to interpret bar charts, pie charts and histograms, do not spend much time on them.
  4. (Remark:Again: You will not be required to construct any types of charts by hand, in an exam situation.)
