Statistics for Quality Sciences
Unit 5
Week 1 Targets We start the program by looking at Units 5, which deals with Descriptive statistics.
We should be able to do the following:
  • Compute the sample mean
  • Be able to specify the sample size "n"
  • compute the sample median for various sample sizes.
  • Identify Skewed Data (Data with Outliers)
  • Compute the Sample Variance
  • Compute the Sample Standard Deviation.
  • State the maximum, minimum and Range
  • Compute the First and Third Quartile (Q1 and Q3)
    • Remark : This material follows from medians
  • Compute the Inter-Quartile Range
  • Construct Boxplots
In advance of unit 6, you should also be able to
  • Compute Z scores
  • Use the Murdoch Barnes Table 3 to determine probabilities
  • State the complement rule for Normal Probabilities
Unit 5 - Numerical Methods

Learning outcomes that relate to an exam situation: 1. Measures of Centrality
  • I will require you to know how to compute a sample mean and sample median, using a hand-held calculator.
  • (Remark: You will not be required to compute a trimmed mean. It is good to understand what it is for though).
  • (Remark: the mode is useful to know, but not particularly important for this module)


2. Measures of Dispersion
  • I will require you to know how to compute a sample variance and standard deviation, using a hand-held calculator.
  • I will require you to know how to compute the range, the quartiles (Q1 and Q3) and the inter-quartile range.
  • (Remark: Z-scores are useful to know now, but really we will be using them in a later unit.)


3. Boxplots
  • Using the sample median, Q1 and Q3 and the IQR, be able to construct a boxplot.
  • Be able to compute the Upper Fence and Lower Fence .
  • Be able to identify outliers using these fences.
  • You will be expected to be able to draw a boxplot by hand, in an exam situation.
4. Other Topics It is useful to be familiar with a couple of topics from this unit, but do not consider them to be core material for the exam.
  • Outliers and Skewness
  • The mode
  • The trimmed mean
  • Tukey's Five Number Summary
