Targets for Weeks 1 and 2

  1. The first units that we will look at are: Units 3, 4 and 5
  2. Aside from Minitab learning outcomes, sections 3 and 4 really just require some reading and comprehension.

Unit 3 - Theory Elements

There are several theoretical concepts and definitions in this section. It is important to have a clear idea of the following definitions.
  1. Be able to distinguish between a population and a sample.
  2. Be able to distinguish between parameter values and Statistics, and know how those values are related to populations and samples.
  3. Be able to distinguish between Quantitative data and Qualitative data.
    • (Remark: For our own purposes, conside the differences between Numeric data and Categorical data.)
  4. Be able to distinguish between discrete variables and continuous variables (i.e. counts vs measurements).
Remark: Scales of Measurement are useful to know, but I am not placing as much emphasis on that section of unit .

Unit 4 - Graphical Procedures

A lot of Material in this section related to constructing graphics in Minitab.
Aside from Minitab, all I really want for you is to be able to understand and interpret the various types of charts.
  1. I would like you to be able to understand a statistical frequency table (see: Table 4.2 Blemish / Flash etc).
  2. (Remark: You will not be required to construct such a table by hand, in an exam situation.)
  3. While I would like you to be able to interpret bar charts, pie charts and histograms, do not spend much time on them.
  4. (Remark: Again, you will not be required to construct any types of charts by hand, in an exam situation.)


Unit 5 - Numerical Methods


Learning outcomes that relate to an exam situation: 1. Measures of Centrality
  • I will require you to know how to compute a sample mean and sample median, using a hand-held calculator.
  • (Remark: You will not be required to compute a trimmed mean. It is good to understand what it is for though).
  • (Remark: the mode is useful to know, but not particularly important for this module)


2. Measures of Dispersion
  • I will require you to know how to compute a sample variance and standard deviation, using a hand-held calculator.
  • I will require you to know how to compute the range, the quartiles (Q1 and Q3) and the inter-quartile range.
  • (Remark: Z-scores are useful to know now, but really we will be using them in a later unit.)


3. Boxplots
  • Using the sample median, Q1 and Q3 and the IQR, be able to construct a boxplot.
  • Be able to compute the Upper Fence and Lower Fence .
  • Be able to identify outliers using these fences.
  • You will be expected to be able to draw a boxplot by hand, in an exam situation.
4. Other Topics (Not Examinable) It is useful to be familiar with a couple of topics from this unit, but do not consider them to be core material for the exam.
  • Outliers and Skewness
  • The mode
  • The Trimmed Mean
  • Tukey's Five Number Summary
